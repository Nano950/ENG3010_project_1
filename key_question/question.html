Key Question in the Field

RQ: How will the introduction of artificial intelligence into the workplace impace softaware developers?


Within the software development discourse community, one major conversation point in how the introduction of artificial intelligence into the software development life cycle will impact the jobs of existing and future developers. This question is also personally relevant as I plan on engaging in software development in a professional capacity, therefore having an understanding of how the integration of a new and potentially disruptive technology may shape my job prospects in the future. An understanding of how Artificial intelligence will shape the workforce of software development in the future will also allow me to better adapt to an ever-changing environment while gaining an understanding of how to better optimize my learning and workflow.



Source

Kia, W. (2024). The sudden disruptive rise of generative artificial intelligence? An evaluation of their impact on higher education and the global workplace. Journal of Open Innovation Technology Market and Complexity, 10(2), 100278–100278. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.joitmc.2024.100278

Annotation

This academic article describes the potential for generative AI such as ChatGPT to impact the workplace and education, along with AI’s potential to enhance productivity as well as the challenges of integrating AI into the workplace and higher education. The Author of the article is PhD associate professor at the SP Jain School of Global Management. He is the sole author of the paper and has not stated conflicting interests between the contents of the article. This article is relevant to my research question as it provides insight into the various barriers that prevent AI in the workplace of software development.



Source

Zirar, A., Ali, S. I., & Islam, N. (2023). Worker and workplace Artificial Intelligence (AI) coexistence: Emerging themes and research agenda. Technovation, 124, 102747. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.technovation.2023.102747

Annotation

This source explores the emerging coexistence between workers and artificial intelligence in workplaces, emphasizing four primary themes: workers' distrust of AI due to job security concerns, AI's potential to augment human abilities, the need for technical, human, and conceptual skills, and the ongoing requirement for upskilling and reskilling. All of the authors of this article are professors at various universities across the world, and while there is no explicit mention of conflicting interests, there is also no statement declaring otherwise, which may suggest that a conflict of interest may exist between the content of the work and the organization funding this research. This source is relevant to my research question as it addresses how AI will change various aspects of the workplace as well as addressing the use and importance of various skills.



Source

Bankins, S., Hu, X., & Yuan, Y. (2024). Artificial intelligence, workers, and the future of work skills. Current Opinion in Psychology, 58, 101828–101828. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.copsyc.2024.101828

Annotation

This academic article describes the potential impact AI can have on work, as well as how the introduction of AI into the workplace will change the importance placed on certain skills. Two of the three authors are PhD students, which indicates a high level of knowledge regarding the concepts discussed. While the authors of this article were funded by scholarships granted by the China Scholarship Council as well as the Beijing Institute of Technology, there was no declared conflict of interest between the authors and supporters of this article. This source is relevant as it provides insights into the ways in which AI can impact working professionals, which can be applied to software developers.



Source

Lei, H., Lai, W., Feaster, W., & Chang, A. C. (2023). Artificial intelligence and agile project management. Elsevier EBooks, 401–405. https://doi.org/10.1016/b978-0-323-90534-3.0001

Annotation

This source discusses how the integration of artificial intelligence in agile project management will impact data science projects within the field of health care. The authors of this article consist of data scientists and healthcare professionals such as cardiologists and healthcare information officers, which indicates that the authors have the knowledge and experiences within subjects relevant to the article to speak as voices of authority. This article is relevant to my research question as it describes how AI impacts project management of software projects as well as AI’s impacts on a specific subset of software development projects, which can contribute to a more holistic view of how AI will impact the workforce of software development.



Source

Bhandari, K., Kumar, K., & Sangal, A. L. (2023). Artificial Intelligence in Software Engineering: Perspectives and Challenges. 2023 Third International Conference on Secure Cyber Computing and Communication (ICSCCC), 133–137. https://doi.org/10.1109/icsccc58608.2023.10176436

Annotation

This article discusses the potential for the introduction of artificial intelligence into software engineering to improve and streamline phases of the software development life cycle. All the authors of this article have academic backgrounds in computer engineering, which afford them the necessary ethos to speak as voices of authority on the concepts within the paper. This article is relevant to my research question as it identifies how AI transforms SE processes, particularly for software developers, by introducing automation and new skill requirements. The article provides discussions on the dual impact of AI in enhancing productivity while presenting new technical and ethical challenges in software development.



Source

Ways of Applying Artificial Intelligence in Software Engineering. (2018). Ways of Applying Artificial Intelligence in Software Engineering. Ieee.org. https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8452880

Annotation

This research article proposes the use of a taxonomy for classifying the levels of automation within AI applications, as well as discusses how AI can be integrated into software engineering processes. The authors of the article all work as professors of computer science within the university of Gothenburg, which suggests that they have knowledge required to speak as voices of authority on the concepts within the article. This article is relevant to my research question as it systematically analyzes AI's transformative impact on SE practices, including its risks and benefits for software developers. The article will also describes how developers can navigate the challenges of adopting AI-driven tools while leveraging their potential to improve software engineering process.
