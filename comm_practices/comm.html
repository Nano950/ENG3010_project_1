Research Article

An exploratory study in communication in Agile Global Software Development


The structure and tone of this research paper indicate a highly formalized style of writing, as it begins with an abstract of the journal's contents. The sections of the paper are consistent with the expectations of a research paper, beginning with the methodology of the study, with descriptions of the research environment, data collected, and discussions of data Throughout the article, the tone of the writer remains impersonal throughout the article, which appeals to the expectations to the academic writing genre. The article discussed more efficient ways for software development teams to communicate during agile sprints. The study found that the use of various technologies such as smart boards was helpful, however, it is important to note that this study was written in 2016, and is not reflective of the current landscape of technology used by the agile development team.


One way that the authors of this article ensure that the content within remains relevant to the readers of the article is by adopting an organization style that begins with a summary of the purpose of the paper, as well as the findings of the study. This method of organization ensures that the readers are aware of the contents of the article, and can make the determination of whether or not the article is relevant. The authors of the article rely on empirical analysis to make the determination that smartboards were beneficial to communication between developers in agile software development.

Forum

Stack Overflow


The content within Stack Overflow is user-generated based on questions asked by other users. Within each question asked, all user responses are listed below the original questions sorted based on the number of upvotes each response to the question receives. As the content of the questions along with responses to questions are written by a large variety of developers at varying levels of expertise, the tone and writing styles vary greatly. There are some indications of more informal tones through the frequent use of contractions; however, the style of writing across both questions asked and the answer remains direct and focused on delivering information.


For questions asked, user-generated content follows a very specific style of organization. Most questions begin by stating a highly specific problem that a user is facing. The question then may include some visual representation of the problem, mostly by including images of the code that the user suspects is resulting in the problem along with any relevant code in other files or sections of a project. Answers from users follow a similar format: they begin by stating what part of the code that the initial user shared is causing a bug or an error, and either follow this description by displaying the corrected code, or some form of content that can be quickly copied and pasted into the initial project. This style of organization allows users attempting to answer questions to quickly understand the nature of the problem while also allowing the users asking questions to easily comprehend and apply the solutions offered by other users.
